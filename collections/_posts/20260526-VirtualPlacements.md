A placement is a great opportunity for researchers and RTPs to learn from each other and establish collaborations. 
However, not everyone can commit to working from a different place for an extended amount of time. 
Family commitments, physical or mental health or disability issues, financial considerations or other reasons can mean that someone misses out on this opportunity.

Virtual placements - while not offering the same complete absorption as an in-person placement - offer a good alternative. However, what you might not need to worry about
in terms of office keys and a desk, you need to invest into making sure that your placee is properly integrated into the team, and has as much facetime with team members
as possible.

[Our guidance for virtual placements on CAKEbox](https://www.cake.ac.uk/CAKEbox/collaboration-and-community-building/virtual_placements/) tries to give you a starting
point to properly plan such a placement. And the [CAKE flexible fund for placements and visits](https://www.cake.ac.uk/about/placements/) can help you with covering 
costs around one-day visits or joint conferences with your virtual visitor, which will enhance the placement experience.
